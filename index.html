CDPH Guidelines for Reopening Counties

In order for counties to move onto the next reopening stage they must meet at least one of the following criteria for the number of new cases, AND at least one of the following criteria for hospitalizations.

Covid Cases per Capita

This chart shows the number of new cases per 100,000 residents in the county over the last two weeks. In order to pass the reopen critera the values should on average be below the yellow dotted line

COVID Patient Influx Rate

This chart shows the 7-day average of percent change of COVID patients in hospitals in the county. Negative numbers mean decreasing. In order to pass reopen critera these values must ALWAYS be below the yellow line

Covid Statistics by County

This chart shows each county in California with it's Covid-19 cases and deaths, as well as normalized per 100,000 residents. The heatmap on the right shows the average number of newly reported cases over the previous 14 days. Areas with an increasing number of cases are shaded darker for larger increases when adjusted for population.

California COVID-19 Testing

This chart shows testing accross all of California. It is important to consider that although cases are increasing, testing is also increasing. If precent tests are increasing at the same rate as cases we aren't testing enough. Keep these in mind while looking at other charts
